Project Description

Declarative Learning-based Programming Framework for the Integration of Domain Knowledge in Statistical Learning (DomiKnowS).
Image

Our ambitious objective is to find is to find breakthrough and novel abstractions for designing AI complex systems based on data and knowledge representation rather than based on underlying computational units. The targeted abstraction layer will be high-level and intuitive for the domain experts to interact with, allowing them to design intelligent models rapidly without worrying about the underlying computations.The framework will allow to seamlessly integrate knowledge about the world and goals expressed via declarative knowledge representation models and procedural knowledge about tasks in training and decision making of statistical and Neural models. We are after a new formalism that can handle uncertainty in various forms including soft constraints, preferences or use partial information while addressing scalability of computations. This is a basic research project supported by ONR.


Team Members

Team behind DomiKnows.
Image

Parisa Kordjamshidi is leading this project. She is working on Relational Machine Learning, Integration of domain knowledge in various forms into learning and Natural Language Processing.

James Allen and his team on natural language understanding and broad coverage of semantics collaborate on this project for integration of linguistic ontologies and semantic parsing in machine learning for natural language understanding.

Choh Man Teng is working on integration of TRIPS ontology in our knowledge based training.

Quan Guo works on designing graph-based deep learning and reasoning.

Andrzej Uszok work on ontology compilation, logical reasoning and constrained programming.

Brent Kristen Venable is working on integration of soft and hard constraints and preferences in machine learning.
